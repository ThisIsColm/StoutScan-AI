Irish web app uses "AI" to analyse your pint — but secretly roasts you instead

The parody app launches ahead of St Patrick's Day and captures a selfie of users trying to photograph their pint.

Dublin, Ireland — March 2026

A new Irish web app launching for St Patrick's Day is taking aim at one of the country's most common pub habits: taking photos of your pint.

The site — StoutScan AI — pretends to use artificial intelligence to analyse your pint of Guinness. Users are prompted to hold their phone steady while the system performs a detailed "scan" of the pint. Progress bars fill. AI jargon appears. The app claims to be analysing head density, pour symmetry, and colour balance.

But while users think they're scanning their pint, the app is actually using the front-facing camera to capture a photo of them — concentrating intensely on their drink.

After the scan finishes, the results page reveals the selfie along with a sarcastic verdict like:

"Analysis complete: It's a pint. Stop taking photos and drink it."

The final result is a shareable image users can download or post to social media.

What Is StoutScan AI?

StoutScan AI is a tongue-in-cheek web app built entirely in the browser. It mimics the look and feel of a genuine AI-powered analysis tool — complete with bounding boxes, confidence scores, progress bars, and an alarming amount of fake machine-learning jargon — but its sole purpose is to take the piss.

There's no actual AI. No model. No neural network. Just vibes, front-end trickery, and a front-facing camera doing the Lord's work.

How It Works

  • User opens the app and points their phone at their pint
  • The app displays a realistic-looking scanning interface with live "analysis" steps
  • While the user is distracted watching fake progress bars, the front camera silently takes a selfie
  • After the analysis "completes," the app reveals the selfie with a sarcastic roast
  • The user can download or share the result

Why It Was Made

The project is a satire of two things happening at once: the relentless hype around AI, and the very Irish obsession with photographing pints as though they're cultural artefacts.

In a world where every product claims to be "powered by AI," StoutScan AI asks the important question: what if we used all this technology just to tell someone to drink their pint?

The overall message is simple: stop taking pictures of your pint and just drink it.

Creator Quote

"It's basically a piss-take of both AI hype and the obsession with photographing pints. I wanted to build something that felt genuinely convincing for about thirty seconds — just long enough for someone to get properly invested before the rug pull."

— Creator of StoutScan AI
